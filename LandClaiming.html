Step 1 : Getting Started

Prior to being able to create your new land! , you must first aquire a minimal of £500 (in-game currency) in order to create your claim. This money can be achieved via setting yourself jobs, completing server quests, voting or completing tasks for other players. One this money is aquired, you can now have the option to create a land.This is done by using the following command: 

/l create (name)
Upon executing this command, the chunk you executed within will turn into your first private claim , created by you!

Step 2 : Claiming Land

▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬ With a feather of choice , select both corners of an area you wish to claim , use left and right click to acomplish this. Upon this you will want to do the following command: 

/l claim
Upon executing this command, the chunk you are standing within or chunks you have selected will further be added upon your land , expanding your claim overall. 
IN ORDER EXTEND CLAIM , THE CHUNKS YOU WISH TO CLAIM MUST BE JOIN/CONNECTED TO A PRE-EXISTING CLAIM

Step 4 : Trusting Players

▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬ The ability to trust players grants other players access to build and utlise your claim, this can be done via the following command: 

/l trust "player" [area or the whole land]
Following this , you can give people the ability to use all or a section of your land by adding the name of an area upon your land , or enter the name of the land to have access to all of it. Upon sending the invite to trust, a player can accept this via going to the invite section of the menu and accepting the invite that way.This can be done via the following command: 

 /l invite
Upon this, you can via all invites given.
Clicking on either the "accept" or "deny" will provoke a response, accepting the invite will allow you to be trusted on the area or entire land of the player who invited you,or you can revoke this offer.Furthermore showing the weekly tax of being registered as a member of the land. If you wish to untrust a player, simple execute the following command: 

 /l untrust "player" [area or the whole land]
Alternatively you can open your land menu , go to your member list, and click the untrust player button for that player.Revoking their membership or ability to use an area or the entire land.

Step 4 : Player Permissions/Roles

▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬▬ If you wish to allow your members to have specfic roles and abilities within your land, this can be done by navigating to player roles , editing them to your suiting then applying these roles to your members.You can consider these roles as "ranks" within your land itself. To access this , previously seen was the "roles" section on the lands menu GUI , simply click this to navigate to said section.

Upon doing so you will be greeted to this GUI:
Seen here are the default roles present,clicking a role of choice will give you the ability to edit the role, changing a variety of attributes of a role, such as:
  • Action Flags - Ability to toggle breaking actions of a variety of blocks ect.
  • Management Flags - Toggles administration flags , claiming , inviting ect.
  • Add members to new areas - Allows the role to invite players.
  • Rename Role.
  • Change the icon of the role.
  • View Role Members.

    • Upon this , you can further delete or add new roles , change the hierarchy of roles.It is entirely up to you, I suggest messing around getting adjusted to this.If you wish to change/set the Roles of players, you can do this via the trusted players section , left clicking to promote them to the next role in the hierarchy you created and right clicking to demote.
